Project Description

EvalQA: Intelligent Chatbot for Question-based Analysis

EvalQA is an advanced chatbot designed to generate questions based on given text. The platform currently supports two types of questions:

1. Multiple Choice Question (MCQ): These questions include a correct answer and several distractors, making them suitable for reflective evaluations.

2. Simple Question/Answer (Q&A): These questions support more in-depth discussions and exploration.

Model Development

Figure 1: Model Architecture

To ensure the highest level of accuracy and functionality, our system incorporates two highly influential transformer-based models: T5 for question generation and Pegasus for text summarization

The process involves the following common steps:

  1. Summarization: Summarization plays a crucial role in formulating the context by utilizing different words while retaining the essential ideas.
  2. Answer Extraction: This step focuses on extracting key and important sentences from the given text, which serve as the basis for generating correct responses.
  3. Question Generation: Leveraging the power of T5, a model developed by Google, we generate questions based on the true sentence and the corresponding correct answer. To fine-tune T5 for this task, we utilized our proprietary dataset, which includes manually collected exam extracts as well as data from the SQuAD dataset.

MCQ Generation:

Our website presents a cutting-edge MCQ generation model that extends the capabilities of the question-answer model. This MCQ model follows a three-step process to generate word-level answer questions:

  1. Keyword Extraction: We extract salient keywords from the given text, identifying the most relevant information.
  2. Keyword Filtering: Inappropriate choices are carefully filtered out based on the extracted keywords, ensuring the quality and relevance of the options.
  3. Distractor Generation: Plausible distractors are generated by identifying related concepts to the keywords, providing challenging yet plausible incorrect choices.
